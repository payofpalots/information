Sending & Requesting Money

Getting familiar with us this article will help you understand sending or requesting money on Venmo.

 

How can I send a payment or request money with Venmo?

Here’s how to send or request money:

  1. Tap Pay/Request at the bottom of the screen
  2. Search and add the person you want to
  3. Enter an amount and add a note
  4. Tap Request or Pay

 

How can I pay multiple people at once?

To pay more than one person at a time, you'll need to add everyone as a recipient before sending your payment.

Here's how:

  1. Tap the Pay/Request button at the bottom of your screen
  2. Search for or tap on the name of your first recipient
  3. Next to the first recipient’s name, tap the plus icon (+)
  4. Search for and select your remaining payment partners
  5. Tap "Next" at the bottom of your screen
  6. Enter the amount of money you’d like to pay each person and a payment note
  7. Tap "Pay" or "Request"

There are a few things you'll want to remember about sending payments to more than one person:

  • The same payment note will be visible to all of each your payment partners
  • You'll want to make separate payments for differing payment notes

 

Do I need to add money to Venmo to pay someone?

Nope! You don’t need to add money to Venmo to make payments. If you're unsure about which payment method will be used for a specific payment, you can always check the bottom of the screen where you enter all your payment details to see which payment method will be used.

Learn more about money in your Venmo account and Venmo balance.

If you don't see Venmo Balance as an option, you'll need to verify your identity. Learn more about verifying your identity on Venmo.

 

I’m having trouble splitting a payment or request.

Before sending the payment, you can edit the total or the individual amounts to make sure each person receives the amount you want. If you receive an error message the amounts you added may not match, clear out your inputs and try again.

Payment from a Stranger

If you received a payment from someone you don’t know, read on to learn what to do.

 

Receiving a payment from a stranger

If you receive a payment from someone you don't know, please contact our Support team. We can help you reverse this payment. If you receive a payment request from a stranger, you should decline it.   

To ensure this doesn’t happen again, you can block the user who paid you or sent you a request. We also recommend checking your privacy settings.    

Remember: When someone pays you on Venmo, that person does not have access to your payment methods (for example: your bank account information or your debit card number).

 

Does receiving a payment from a stranger mean my Venmo account has been hacked? 

If you haven’t received unexpected notifications from Venmo or notice any unauthorized transactions in your account, probably not. Because we offer a search feature for accounts, occasionally Venmo users may pay strangers by mistake (often with the same name as their friend). In those cases, it’s best to contact us to report that you were paid in error, then block the user to prevent the issue from happening in the future.

It is not possible to cancel a payment to an existing Venmo account. Once a payment is sent, the funds are available to the recipient right away.

 

There’s an issue with a payment I sent

If you sent a payment to someone you know and need it returned, please have the recipient send you a payment for the same amount. Upon return, you can transfer those funds from your Venmo account to your bank. If you accidentally paid your friend twice or made a duplicate payment, have the recipient pay you back for one of the payments. 

Venmo Support can only reverse a payment if:

  1. The recipient gives their explicit permission
  2. Their account is in good standing
  3. They still have the funds available in their Venmo account.

Venmo Support cannot reverse a payment at the sender’s request.

My payment is listed as “Pending” 

If you sent a payment to a phone number or email address that has not been registered with a Venmo account yet, you can take back the payment in the “Pending” section of the Venmo app. See this article for more details. 

Note: Payments sent via iMessage work a bit differently. Check out this article if you sent your payment via iMessage.  

 

There’s an issue with a payment I received

Before taking any action, we recommend checking the profile that sent you the payment. Make sure the person who sent the money was actually your friend.

If someone you know sent you a payment by mistake, or if they made a mistake in the payment, sending the money back to them typically resolved the matter. Payments on Venmo can’t be cancelled once they’ve reached your Venmo account. 

If the full amount you received is still in your Venmo account, that money will be used to fund your payment back to them—assuming you have access to a Venmo balance. If you don’t have access to a Venmo balance, or if the full amount isn’t in your Venmo account, the payment will be funded with the payment method you select.
